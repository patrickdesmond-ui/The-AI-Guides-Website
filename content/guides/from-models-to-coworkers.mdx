February was a big month for anyone watching the frontier labs.

OpenAI launched [Frontier](https://openai.com/index/introducing-openai-frontier/), its new enterprise platform for building and managing AI agents. Anthropic expanded [Claude Cowork](https://www.anthropic.com/product/claude-cowork) with Windows support, scheduled tasks, and a fresh set of [role-specific plug-ins](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/24/anthropic-launches-new-push-for-enterprise-agents-with-plugins-for-finance-engineering-and-design/) for finance, legal, HR and design.

Both are pointing at the same thing. Moving AI out of the chat window and into the real workflows your team runs every day.

Here's what's new, and what you can do with it today.

## What is Frontier?

OpenAI is pitching Frontier as a platform for "AI coworkers" — their phrase. The idea is you onboard an agent the way you'd onboard a new employee. Give it context. Grant it permissions. Teach it the work. Review its output. Give it feedback.

The technical layer doing this is what the industry calls orchestration. It sits between the model and your business — plugging into your CRM, data warehouse, ticketing tools, SharePoint, and the internal apps your team already uses — so agents operate with real business context instead of a blank prompt.

As of early March 2026, Frontier is in limited preview and sales-led, aimed squarely at large enterprises.

## What is Cowork?

Claude Cowork is Anthropic's version of the same idea, approached differently. Cowork runs on your desktop. It reads and writes to your local files, moves between apps, and handles multi-step tasks end-to-end. Give it a goal, come back to a finished output.

As of early March 2026, Cowork is in research preview and available on all paid Claude plans (from $20/month). Windows support landed in February, scheduled tasks a few weeks later.

The new plug-ins turn Claude into a specialist for a given function. Each one bundles skills, connectors and sub-agents tuned for that kind of work — legal gets contract review and NDA triage, finance gets reconciliations and variance analysis, and so on.

## What can they do?

A few examples that map onto real SME work:

**Weekly reporting.** Pull metrics from your analytics dashboard every Friday, combine with commentary from a Notion doc, drop the finished report in a specific folder, Slack the team a summary link.

**Client onboarding.** Read the signed contract, pull relevant info into your CRM, generate welcome documents from your template, schedule the kickoff call.

**Inbox triage.** Scan incoming emails each morning, categorise them, draft replies for the routine ones, flag the rest with suggested next actions.

**Finance close.** Pull the month's transactions, reconcile against invoices in your document folder, generate the variance commentary, send the draft to the accountant.

None of these is science fiction in 2026. They run on tools you can sign up for this afternoon.

## How to get started

For most SMEs, Cowork is the easier entry point. Subscription-based, runs from the Claude desktop app, no enterprise deployment required. You can test it on a single workflow this week.

Frontier is a different category — enterprise-first and out of reach for most SMEs today, though the patterns it's built on are flowing through the whole industry.

The mistake I'd avoid is trying to boil the ocean. Pick one workflow. Define the boundary. Give the agent [scoped access](/executive-guide-to-ai-governance-sme-edition) to the right folders or systems — not everything. Check the output for a couple of weeks. Adjust. Then scale to the next workflow. If you're not sure where to start, [AI Strategy Essentials for SMEs](/ai-strategy-essentials-for-smes) covers how to identify and prioritise your first use cases.
